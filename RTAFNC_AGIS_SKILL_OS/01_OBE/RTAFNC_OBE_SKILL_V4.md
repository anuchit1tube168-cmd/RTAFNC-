# RTAFNC OBE / Sub-PLO Skill V4

## Mission
Build an OBE system for RTAFNC that is aligned with nursing standards, professional ethics, military-air-force context, and quality assurance evidence.

## Core Identity
RTAFNC does not produce general civilian nurses and does not produce combat officers. It produces Air Force nursing graduates with professional nursing competence, humanized care, military discipline, basic military skills, military nursing leadership, and readiness to support Air Force medical missions.

## Military Nursing Indicators
| Code | Indicator |
|---|---|
| 2.1 Military Discipline | Follow military discipline, customs, chain of command, and safety requirements correctly. |
| 2.2 Basic Military Skills | Demonstrate basic military skills, reporting, uniform discipline, and official conduct correctly and safely. |
| 2.3 Military Nursing Leadership | Demonstrate leader/follower roles, teamwork, problem solving, and coordination in training and simulation. |
| 2.4 Military Medical Support | Integrate nursing, basic military skills, casualty care, triage, and medical referral for military medical support. |
| 2.5 Aerospace / Aeromedical Nursing | Perform air-force and aviation nursing care in relevant simulation or mission contexts. |
| 2.6 Military Professionalism | Demonstrate discipline, endurance, sacrifice, honesty, responsibility, service mind, and military professional ethics. |

## Progression: Military Training 1-4
| Year | Focus | Expected Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Year 1 Foundation | discipline, military customs, drill, leave documents, route march | perform basic military conduct correctly, safely, and honestly |
| Year 2 Team Member | followership, team, responsibility, endurance | work with others and communicate in a military system |
| Year 3 Team Leader | small-team leadership, problem solving, coordination, scenarios | plan, coordinate, and lead small-team activities or simulated missions |
| Year 4 Integrator | nursing + military + Air Force mission integration | make decisions in simulation and support military medical missions |

## SOS150 Adaptation Rule
Use SOS150 only as a leadership framework: leadership, problem solving, coordination, and military ethics. Do not copy command-level language that exceeds undergraduate nursing scope.

## Assessment Blueprint
- Direct observation + checklist: drill and military customs
- Behavioral rubric: discipline, punctuality, responsibility
- Document-based assessment: leave reports and military documents
- Peer + instructor rubric: leadership, followership, teamwork
- Simulation assessment: aerospace medicine, aviation nursing, disaster response
- Portfolio + reflection: ethics, service mind, professional growth

## Definition of Done
OBE work is complete only when it has measurable PLO/Sub-PLO, linked YLO/CLO, assessment blueprint, rubric, evidence register, CQI loop, and remaining gap/risk list.
